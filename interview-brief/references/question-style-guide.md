# Question Style Guide — JRE + All-in 融合風格

The user wants the question bank to feel like **Joe Rogan Experience** and **All-in Podcast** had a baby, adapted for a Chinese/bilingual interview setting. This file is where the *craft* lives. Read this before drafting any question.

## The two parent styles

### Joe Rogan (JRE)
- **Radical curiosity.** Rogan doesn't pretend to know. "Wait wait wait, what does that mean?" is a legitimate question, not a weakness.
- **Anchored in the specific.** "You said once that…" "I saw a clip where you…" He quotes them back to themselves, which disarms media-trained answers.
- **Let silence happen.** Asks a question, then shuts up. The guest fills the space, often with something unplanned.
- **Willing to go personal fast.** Family, psychedelics, fear, death, physical pain. Not as gotcha — as shared human territory.
- **Will push, but not to humiliate.** Disagrees openly. "That doesn't sound right to me."
- **Digressive on purpose.** Follows tangents because that's where the real stuff lives.

### All-in Podcast (Chamath / Sacks / Friedberg / Jason)
- **Demand specificity.** "What's your number?" "Best case, base case, bear case?" Vague answers are called out.
- **Debate posture.** Takes a position, makes the guest defend theirs. Not neutral.
- **Track record accountability.** "Six months ago you said X. What changed?" Holds them to prior claims.
- **Macro framing.** Any micro story gets pulled up to "what does this mean for the next 18 months?"
- **Insider register.** Uses the guest's own in-group vocabulary rather than translating down.
- **Humor as pressure release.** Self-deprecation, running gags, light mockery of each other. Makes hard questions palatable.

## What to fuse

JRE's **specificity + personal territory** + All-in's **demand for numbers + debate posture** — that's the target.

Avoid: the polite-journalist register ("Tell us about your background"), the LinkedIn-interview register ("What's the biggest lesson you've learned"), and the TED register ("What's your one message for the audience").

## Question types to include in every brief

Aim for roughly this distribution across a 20-30 question bank:

| Type | Count | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 破冰題 Cold Open | 2-3 | Disarm, set register, establish this is not a press tour |
| 故事線題 Narrative | 3-5 | Walk through a specific moment — not "your career" but "the day you decided to leave Goldman" |
| 深挖題 Follow-ups | 4-6 | Second-layer questions pre-written for likely answers to the narrative questions |
| 觀點題 Opinion / Debate | 4-6 | Force a position, with a counter-position ready |
| 情感題 Personal / Emotional | 3-5 | Family, fear, regret, role models, loneliness, ego |
| 反直覺題 Counter-intuitive | 2-3 | Flip their usual framing; quote critics back at them |
| 簽名題 Signature Close | 2-3 | Memorable ending — not "advice for young people" |

## Templates that work

### 破冰題 patterns
- `「我看了你上個月在 {specific venue} 的訪談，你說 {specific claim}。我聽完有個疑問——{sharp follow-up}」` (references something concrete, signals you did homework, pins them to prior words)
- `「我想先問一個跟 {their main topic} 完全無關的問題——{something specific about them personally}」` (Rogan-style disarm)
- `「很多人對你的第一印象是 {public perception}。我想知道，你自己看自己的時候，會用哪三個詞？」` (forces self-description without cliché)

### 故事線題 patterns
- `「把我帶回到 {specific moment / year / decision}。你那天早上起床的時候，已經知道你要做 X 了嗎？」` (JRE "take me back" + specific sensory anchor)
- `「{具體決定} 之前三個月，你的日常長什麼樣子？」` (pre-decision context often more revealing than the decision itself)

### 深挖題 patterns (pre-write these under each narrative question)
- `「等一下，你剛說 {guest's likely phrase}——那個時候你真的這樣想嗎？還是你現在回看才這樣解釋？」` (distinguishes authentic memory from retrospective narrative)
- `「如果當時你做了另一個選擇，三年後你猜你在哪裡？」`
- `「講這件事的時候，你第一個想到的是誰？」` (emotional anchor)

### 觀點題 / debate patterns
- `「{某個 public figure} 跟你立場完全相反，他的 argument 是 {steelman their position}。你會怎麼回應？」` (force them to engage the strongest counter)
- `「給我三個數字——best case / base case / bear case——{specific domain}」` (All-in signature)
- `「未來 18 個月你最怕看到什麼？」` (macro framing)
- `「你六個月前說 {prior claim}。現在還是這樣看嗎？」` (accountability)

### 情感題 patterns
- `「你爸媽知道你現在在做什麼嗎？他們怎麼解釋給你親戚聽？」` (family + social register, often surfaces real feelings about work)
- `「誰是你在凌晨三點睡不著時會想打電話的人？」` (role model / support network, indirect)
- `「你最後一次感覺自己是 impostor 是什麼時候？」`
- `「你成功之後，哪段關係變了？」`
- `「如果你十五歲的自己看到今天的你，他會說什麼？」`

### 反直覺題 patterns
- `「你最被高估的技能是什麼？」` (flip the "what's your superpower" cliché)
- `「你同意你最嚴厲的批評者 {quote specific critic} 哪一部分？」`
- `「有沒有一件你公開說過、但私底下其實沒那麼相信的事？」`

### 簽名題 patterns
- `「如果五年後你不再做這件事了，那會是因為什麼發生了？」`
- `「你希望別人用什麼一句話來誤解你？」` (subverts "how do you want to be remembered")
- `「此刻最讓你睡不著的一個問題是什麼？我不要答案，只要問題本身。」`

## Humor calibration

Match to the guest's tolerance (which you assessed in the *Humor Profile* section):

- **High tolerance** (e.g. founders who roast themselves on X, comedians, crypto personalities): deploy self-mocking setups, running callbacks to earlier answers, light teasing about inconsistencies
- **Medium tolerance**: dry understatement, raised eyebrows, one earnest question about something absurd in their past
- **Low tolerance** (corporate execs, public officials, cultural figures who guard dignity): skip the humor layer; rely on warmth + specificity instead. *Do not force it.*

## Follow-up discipline

For every **narrative** and **opinion** question, pre-write 2-3 follow-ups branching on likely answers. Format:

```
Q: [Main question]
  ↳ If they say A: [follow-up A]
  ↳ If they deflect / give PR answer: [sharper follow-up]
  ↳ If they go personal: [warmer follow-up to keep the vein open]
```

The `if they deflect` branch is the one most often forgotten. Include it every time.

## Questions to explicitly avoid

- "What's your biggest lesson / failure / advice?" (Vague, invites platitudes)
- "Walk us through your journey." (Gives them their well-rehearsed narrative)
- Anything that can be answered with "it depends" without the guest feeling the need to say more
- Yes/no questions without a follow-up ladder
- Questions where the guest has a pre-canned answer you can find in their last 3 interviews — unless you're using it as a *setup* to challenge the pre-canned answer

## One final heuristic

Before finalizing the question bank, read it and ask: **"Would this make the guest laugh, flinch, or think for 5 seconds before answering?"** If nothing in the bank provokes any of those three reactions, the interview will be forgettable. Rewrite until the bank passes this test.
